Scope: Disease Forecast And Simulation app

SECTION: PROJECT OVERVIEW
The Disease Forecast And Simulation is an uncertainty-aware forecasting dashboard built with Streamlit.
It supports outbreak forecasting, forecast validation, intervention comparison, agent-based simulation, and FAQ assistance.
The forecasting workflow is probabilistic and uses multiple simulations to represent uncertainty.

SECTION: KEY EPIDEMIOLOGY TERMS
R0 (basic reproduction number): average number of secondary infections from one infectious person in a fully susceptible population.
Interpretation:
- R0 > 1: outbreak tends to grow
- R0 = 1: outbreak is stable
- R0 < 1: outbreak declines

SEIR states:
- S: Susceptible
- E: Exposed (infected but not yet infectious)
- I: Infectious
- R: Recovered

SEIRD extension:
- D: Dead
The app uses IFR to split I to R or D.

IFR (Infection Fatality Rate): fraction of all infected individuals who die.
In the model, when infectious individuals leave I:
- with probability IFR, transition to D
- with probability 1 - IFR, transition to R

SECTION: FORECASTING METHOD
The app uses a hybrid ensemble strategy for comparison forecasting and probabilistic outputs.
Typical structure includes:
- Renewal-style component
- Trend/statistical component
- Mechanistic compartment component
Outputs are generated from repeated stochastic simulations and displayed as mean plus uncertainty interval.

Why forecasts show a range:
- random transmission events
- parameter uncertainty
- under-reporting and observation noise
- changing epidemic dynamics

SECTION: VALIDATION
Validation compares forecasted values with real-world data over aligned dates.
Common metrics shown in the app:
- MAE (Mean Absolute Error)
- RMSE (Root Mean Square Error)
- MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error)
- Bias
- Coverage of prediction interval
- Correlation

Coverage interpretation:
If using an 80 percent interval, ideal coverage is around 80 percent.
- Too low coverage: intervals too narrow
- Too high coverage: intervals too wide

SECTION: INTERVENTIONS
Interventions reduce effective spread and alter outbreak trajectory.
Examples:
- Masks
- Distancing
- Lockdown
- Vaccination
Intervention comparison reports expected reduction in peak, cases, and mortality-related impact.

SECTION: AGENT SIMULATION
The agent simulation provides an artificial-life style outbreak visualization.
Modes:
- Curve-guided mode: follows target epidemic curve
- Custom/emergent mode: behavior emerges from interactions

Agent colors:
- Green: Susceptible
- Yellow: Exposed
- Red: Infectious
- Blue: Recovered
- Black: Dead

SECTION: EXAMPLE Q AND A SNIPPETS
Q: Why is forecast shown as a band and not one line?
A: Because outbreak evolution is stochastic and uncertain. The band summarizes multiple simulations.

Q: Why can projected deaths be higher than estimated deaths from real data?
A: If forecasted case counts are higher than actual counts, applying the same IFR yields higher projected deaths.

Q: Why changing IFR does not heavily change infection curve?
A: IFR mainly controls I to R versus I to D transitions. Transmission is driven mostly by spread parameters.
